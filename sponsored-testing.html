How it works

How a sponsored testing program works

A turnkey, end-to-end process — from program design and HCP deployment to sequencing, results delivery, and real-world data generation.

01

Define Program Scope & Targets

Collaborate with Helix to define therapeutic areas, genetic targets, eligibility criteria, and program goals — whether focused on patient identification, data generation, or both.

02

Deploy HCP Ordering & Engagement Tools

Launch co-branded ordering workflows with EHR-integrated Best Practice Alerts, a dedicated physician portal, and targeted HCP marketing to drive program adoption across health system partners.

03

Sequence & Link Clinical Data

Patients receive Exome+® whole exome sequencing at no cost. Results are linked to longitudinal EHR data — labs, diagnoses, medications, and 13+ years of clinical history — via OMOP-harmonized data structures.

04

Deliver Actionable Results

Clinicians and patients receive results via secure portal access. Genetic counseling is offered for actionable findings, and patients are connected to specialists and care pathways in line with current workflows.

05

Generate & Deliver Clinicogenomic Data

Sponsors receive consented, de-identified clinico-genomic data — WES VCF, matched longitudinal clinical records, OMOP-harmonized — via real-time dashboards and a dedicated data license.

Use Case — Rare Disease patient identification and data generation

Sponsored WES program drives rare disease diagnosis and builds a clinicogenomic research asset

A biopharma partner sponsored a whole-exome sequencing program targeting a rare genetic disease through Helix's health system network. Leveraging both physician-initiated ordering and population genomics recall, the program identified previously undiagnosed patients, delivered actionable clinical results, and generated a matched clinicogenomic dataset — including WES VCF, longitudinal EHR, and OMOP-harmonized data — for translational and discovery research.

The combination of EHR-integrated ordering and population-level recall allowed us to identify patients who would never have been tested through traditional clinical pathways — while generating a research-grade data asset in parallel.
